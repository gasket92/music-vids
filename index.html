Genre: Pop Punk

Pop punk is a rock music genre that combines punk rock with pop and power pop. It's known for its fast pace, energetic sound, and relatable lyrics. Not really metal but worth a mention.

City Lights - Hang Out

I was a big fan of these guys back in the day.

Genre: Easycore

Easycore is essentially pop punk that incorporates elements of metalcore, such as breakdowns (I live for breakdowns, I'll point those out) and screaming vocals, while maintaining a catchy and melodic sound.

Abandoned By Bears - Heads or Tails

The breakdown from 02:40 til the end is a good example.

A Day To Remember - The Downfall of Us All

A Day to Remember are one of the most famous and popular bands that do this sort of music but over their career their style has changed a bit.

Genre: Post-Hardcore

Lots of melody and harmony, usually high-pitched singing but with sections that are aggressive with screaming.

Genre: Metalcore

Metalcore is a fusion genre combining extreme metal and hardcore punk, characterized by breakdowns, heavy riffs, double bass drumming, and often a mix of screamed and clean vocals.

Parkway Drive - Pandora

This was my favourite song for a long time. Parkway Drive were my favourite band in my late teens. They're actually massive now. Breakdown at 02:55.

Miss May I - A Dance With Aera Cura

Genre: Deathcore

Deathcore combines death metal with metalcore. The vocalist is often what sets a band apart in this genre. It's a competition to see who can make the most gnarly, horrible sounds. Almost never any clean singing.

Signs of the Swarm - Nightcrawler

Breakdown at 01:46 with some disgusting vocals.

Peacemaker FL - Cease To Exist

These guys are a modern band doing Deathcore like it used to be in the 00s (the glory days).

Genre: Slam

Slam death metal is a subgenre of death metal known for its heavy, low-tuned sound and intense breakdowns. Vocals are usually just gurgly noises, quite one-dimensional in a way.

Desoectomy - Dumptruck Disembodiment

That riff at 0:47 is basically all slam songs ever. Iconic.
